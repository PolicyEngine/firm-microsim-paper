PLACEBO BUNCHING TEST — £85k UK VAT threshold (vintage 2023-24)
======================================================================

Method
------
The HMRC turnover-band targets are coarse: one count for <= £85k
(£1_to_Threshold = 678,350) and one for £85k-£150k
(£Threshold_to_£150k = 305,320). Their average per-£1k densities differ
(7,981 vs 4,697 firms/£1k), so the calibration target ITSELF contains a
density step at exactly £85k. The generator has no firm location-choice,
so any density discontinuity is inherited from this target.

PLACEBO A (reweight): rescale the existing synthetic firms' weights so
the per-£1k turnover density follows a single smooth log-quadratic trend
across £85k (fit on the wings, |y-85|>20k, then applied inside the
window). Total mass over [20k,140k] preserved. No regeneration.

PLACEBO B (regenerate): replace the two HMRC band counts straddling £85k
with a split implied by a SINGLE common per-£1k density (combined mass
spread uniformly over £1-£150k), so the calibration target has NO step,
then re-run the full generator + multi-objective calibration and the
estimator on the resulting weighted population.
