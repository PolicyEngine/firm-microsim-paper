\subsection{Data construction detail}
\label{app:data}

Section~\ref{sec:data} describes the two-stage synthetic-population pipeline
(\texttt{analysis/gen\_full\_param.py}, parameterised by \texttt{VAT\_THRESHOLD})
and its calibration to HMRC and ONS aggregates. This appendix records the
implementation detail deferred from that account.

Each of the approximately $2.94$ million firm rows carries the fields
\texttt{annual\_turnover\_k}, \texttt{annual\_input\_k}, \texttt{weight}, and
\texttt{vat\_registered}; calibration weights are chosen so the weighted totals
reproduce the official targets while the population sums to about $2.0$ million
firms. Because the population is calibrated \emph{to} the HMRC aggregates,
agreement between my weighted outputs and those aggregates is a check of
\emph{internal consistency}, not external validation: any target used in
calibration is reproduced by construction. I therefore read the
$\sim\!87\%$ calibration accuracy, and the close match of the static
\pounds85{,}000\,\(\to\)\,\pounds90{,}000 costing to HMRC's published figures, as
consistency checks on the pipeline rather than out-of-sample tests of firm
behaviour.

\paragraph{Counterfactual exclusion window and polynomial degree.} The no-VAT
counterfactual density of Section~\ref{sec:bunching} is fitted by polynomial
regression on the observed density outside a manipulation window around the
threshold. The baseline excludes $[T^{*}-15\text{k}, T^{*}+15\text{k}]$ and fits
a cubic. Widening the window guards against contamination of the counterfactual
by the behavioural response but reduces the data available for the fit; raising
the degree improves in-sample fit but risks over-fitting the tails. The
sensitivity of the excess-mass and bunching-ratio estimates to window width and
degree, with associated uncertainty, is reported in
Appendix~\ref{app:inference}.

\paragraph{Subsample for dynamic plots.} The dynamic re-optimisation figures
of the companion structural step are generated on a $20\%$ random subsample of the
firm rows, to keep the firm-by-firm FOC inversion and re-solution tractable,
while the static revenue sweep and the calibration use the full dataset. The
$20\%$ subsample reproduces the full-dataset density step and bunching mass to
within sampling error, so subsampling affects only the smoothness of the plotted
curves, not the substantive conclusions.
